Objective

UBC Bionics Engineering Analysis and Research (BEAR) is a new student team, formed in Oct 2018, with the goal of creating a prosthetic hand for competition in CYBATHLON 2020. The project is called GRASP - an affordable, reliable, smart bionic hand.

Despite the ambitious timeline, this is a multi-year project, hence our most senior members are 3rd year students (including myself). Because of this, I've taken on more responsibility with regards to both designing the prosthetic and providing guidance for the younger members.

Main Responsibilities

I work primarily on the wrist subteam, however I also contribute to the finger designs. My main responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

  • Designing 2-DoF wrist mechanism to fit inside forearm enclosure.

  • Calculating part requirements based on load and fatigue criteria.

  • Specifying commercial off-the-shelf components.

  • Performing FEA and thermal analysis in ANSYS/SimScale.

  • Concept generation and evaluation via weighted decision matrix (WDM).

Designs
